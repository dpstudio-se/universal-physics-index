# Contributing to Universal Physics Index

UPI welcomes rigorous contributions from physicists, mathematicians,
programmers, historians of science, and critical readers.

Every new node or bridge must include:

1. A stable UPI address.
2. Explicit symbol definitions and units.
3. Equations or relation rules.
4. A declared physical mechanism.
5. A scientific status: EST, DER, HYP, STOP, ERR, or SYM.
6. Evidence and primary sources.
7. A falsification or failure condition.
8. A confusion guard describing similar-looking concepts that must not be merged.

A hypothesis may be radical. Its status may not be ambiguous.

## Scientific claim checklist

Every new claim must include status, equation/relation, definitions, units, assumptions,
provenance, uncertainty, evidence, test and falsification metadata where applicable, and any
symbolic interpretation. Frequencies require an explicit frequency type.

Do not promote `HYP` to `EST` merely because a claim is elegant, matches a symbolic pattern, is
repeated by several agents, resembles a simulation, or uses a number found in unrelated systems.
Promotion requires relevant evidence, provenance and documented review.
